Breast Cancer Detection with Deep Learning

A detailed comparative analysis of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Vision Transformers (ViT) for mammography image classification.

Jump to Key Results

Problem Statement

Accurate and timely diagnosis of breast cancer from mammography images is critical, but often hindered by the sheer volume of data and the presence of severe class imbalance (many negative cases, fewer positive cases) in diagnostic datasets. We sought a robust solution that minimizes false negatives.

Aim and Strategic Approach

The primary aim was to build a highly sensitive binary classifier to distinguish between positive and negative cancer cases. We focused on a systematic comparison between classical CNN models and modern Transformer architectures to find the best fit for complex medical image features.

  • Model Comparison: Evaluated seven pre-trained models, including VGG-19, ResNet-50, and Vision Transformers (CAIT, ViT).
  • Performance Metrics: Prioritized Recall and F1-score to ensure clinical relevance by reducing the false negative rate.

Dataset and Pre-processing

The RSNA Screening Mammography Breast Cancer Detection dataset (over 54,713 images) was used. The pipeline focused heavily on mitigating class imbalance:

  • Data Balancing: Used the Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM) to intelligently filter out redundant negative images, achieving a balanced dataset while preserving information density.
  • Standardization: Images were resized to 224 times 224 pixels and normalized to fit standard deep learning model requirements.

Proposed Work and Comparative Analysis

All models were fine-tuned over 100 epochs using the Adam Optimizer and Cross-Entropy Loss. The study compared model stability and feature extraction effectiveness on the balanced mammography scans.

CNN Performance Highlights

VGG-19 demonstrated the most robust and stable training among all CNN models, providing a strong baseline for the task.

  • Best F1-score: 0.5736 (VGG-19)
  • Best Recall: 0.6667 (VGG-19)

Vision Transformer Performance Highlights

The attention mechanisms in the Transformer architecture proved superior at capturing long-range spatial dependencies across the images.

  • Highest F1-score: 0.6085 (CAIT)
  • Highest Recall: 0.7600 (CAIT)

Conclusion and Key Results

The comparative study successfully identified the CAIT Transformer as the optimal model. Its superior performance in Recall (sensitivity) is paramount in a medical context, where minimizing the risk of missing a cancer case is the primary objective.

Outcome: Successfully implemented a CAIT-based model that achieved a 76% Recall rate on the test set, demonstrating the high potential of Transformers for complex, high-stakes image classification tasks.
